Comparing Filtering Technologies

DNS and Web Filtering are often confused. In FortiOS 7.6, they work together, but have very different impacts on the "Family Experience."

🌐
DNS Layer

DNS Filtering

Stops the request before it even starts. The browser asks "Where is badsite.com?" and the FortiGate gives a false or empty answer.

➕
Pros:

Fastest method. No browser certificate errors. Works on "dumb" devices like Smart TVs and Game Consoles.

➖
Cons:

All or nothing. You can't block just "Youtube.com/BadChannel"—you have to block all of Youtube.

🕸️
L7 Layer

Web Filtering

Inspects the actual traffic as it flows. It looks at the website addresses and the content inside the pages.

➕
Pros:

High precision. Can block specific pages, scan for files, and enforce Safe Search across different browsers.

➖
Cons:

Certificate friction. Without manual device setup, it triggers "Privacy Warnings" that scare the family.

Stability vs. Perfection

The "Paradox of Choice" in home security: The better the filtering, the more likely you are to break the internet.

Long-Term Impact Comparison

1

The "Set & Forget" Zone

Using DNS NXDOMAIN or Web SNI provides high stability. Most apps work perfectly, and no one calls you to complain about "Privacy Warnings."

2

The "Support Call" Zone

Deep Inspection offers the most perfection but breaks banking apps, streaming services, and mobile updates unless you spend hours bypass-listing them.
